## Essay Question

**Topic:** Corporate Social Responsibility and Firm Performance

**Question:** "Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) is ultimately a strategic tool for profit maximisation rather than a genuine commitment to ethical business practice." Critically evaluate this statement, drawing on stakeholder theory and at least two real-world examples."

---

## Scoring Rubric

| Criteria | **1 - Beginning** | **2 - Developing** | **3 - Proficient** | **4 - Exemplary** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Conceptual Understanding** | CSR undefined or confused with philanthropy; stakeholder theory absent. | CSR defined but stakeholder theory mentioned without being applied. | CSR and stakeholder theory accurately defined and applied; student distinguishes instrumental from normative CSR. | Sophisticated conceptual command; student draws on Freeman's stakeholder model, Carroll's CSR pyramid, or similar frameworks to analyse the statement critically. |
| **Critical Evaluation of the Claim** | Agrees or disagrees without reasoning; no counter-argument. | One perspective presented with a token counter-argument. | Engages genuinely with both sides: strategic/profit-motive view and the view that CSR can reflect genuine values or institutional pressure. | Nuanced, well-reasoned verdict; student considers when CSR is cynical greenwashing vs. transformative, and what conditions produce each outcome. |
| **Use of Examples** | No real-world examples, or examples used anecdotally. | One example cited but not analysed in relation to the argument. | Two relevant examples (e.g., Patagonia, Nike, BP, Unilever) used to illustrate different dimensions of the argument. | Examples are well-chosen, specific, and analysed with depth; student uses them to test the claim rather than merely illustrate it. |
| **Argument & Structure** | No clear thesis; rambling or list-based structure. | Thesis present but inconsistently maintained; paragraphs sometimes drift. | Clear thesis; each paragraph advances the argument with evidence and a link to the question. | Coherent, persuasive argument; student synthesises theory and evidence in a conclusion that is justified, conditional, and proportionate. |

---

### Scoring Guide
* **13–16 Points:** High Distinction — Sophisticated critical analysis with strong theoretical grounding and well-used evidence.
* **9–12 Points:** Pass/Credit — Competent engagement with the statement using relevant theory and examples.
* **5–8 Points:** Developing — Descriptive or one-sided; limited use of theory or real-world evidence.
* **0–4 Points:** Needs Improvement — Fundamental misunderstanding of CSR or stakeholder theory.
