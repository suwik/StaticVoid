## Essay Question

**Topic:** Market Failures and Government Intervention

**Question:** "To what extent is government intervention the most effective solution to market failure? Discuss with reference to at least two types of market failure, and evaluate the potential limitations of government action."

---

## Scoring Rubric

| Criteria | **1 - Beginning** | **2 - Developing** | **3 - Proficient** | **4 - Exemplary** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Definition & Classification** | Market failure types are absent or incorrectly defined. | At least one market failure type defined, but explanation is superficial. | Two or more types (e.g., externalities, public goods, information asymmetry) defined accurately with examples. | All relevant types defined precisely; student draws clear distinctions between them and situates them in real-world contexts. |
| **Evaluation of Intervention** | No evaluation of government action; purely descriptive. | Acknowledges that intervention can work but does not weigh costs or limitations. | Evaluates at least one form of intervention (e.g., taxation, regulation, subsidies) against the market failure it targets, noting at least one limitation. | Balanced, nuanced evaluation of multiple instruments; discusses government failure, unintended consequences, and conditions under which intervention is or isn't warranted. |
| **Use of Economic Theory** | No diagrams, no theoretical framework. | Some reference to theory but misapplied or incomplete. | Correct use of at least one diagram (e.g., negative externality with deadweight loss) or theoretical model to support argument. | Multiple well-labelled diagrams and theoretical models; student uses theory to drive analysis rather than describe it. |
| **Argument & Structure** | No clear thesis; ideas are disorganised. | Thesis present but not consistently maintained throughout. | Clear, structured argument addressing both the question's "to what extent" framing and at least two failure types. | Sophisticated, well-signposted argument; conclusion weighs evidence and arrives at a justified, conditional verdict. |

---

### Scoring Guide
* **13–16 Points:** High Distinction — Demonstrates command of microeconomic theory and evaluative rigour.
* **9–12 Points:** Pass/Credit — Competent handling of the core concepts with some evaluation.
* **5–8 Points:** Developing — Descriptive rather than analytical; limited engagement with theory.
* **0–4 Points:** Needs Improvement — Fundamental misunderstanding of market failure or government intervention.
