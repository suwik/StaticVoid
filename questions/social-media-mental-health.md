## Essay Question

**Topic:** Social Media and Adolescent Mental Health

**Question:** "Evaluate the claim that social media use is a significant cause of declining mental health among adolescents. In your answer, consider both the psychological mechanisms proposed and the methodological limitations of existing research."

---

## Scoring Rubric

| Criteria | **1 - Beginning** | **2 - Developing** | **3 - Proficient** | **4 - Exemplary** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Psychological Theory** | No reference to psychological concepts or mechanisms. | Names a concept (e.g., social comparison, FOMO) without explaining how it links to mental health outcomes. | Accurately explains at least two psychological mechanisms (e.g., upward social comparison, displacement of sleep/activity, cyberbullying) with reference to relevant research. | Integrates multiple theories coherently; distinguishes between passive and active use, and connects mechanisms to specific outcomes (anxiety, depression, body image). |
| **Evaluation of Evidence** | Accepts studies at face value; no methodological critique. | Notes that "studies show" a link but does not question the strength or quality of the evidence. | Identifies at least one methodological issue (e.g., correlation vs. causation, self-report bias, screen-time measurement) and uses it to qualify conclusions. | Systematic evaluation of evidence quality; distinguishes correlational from experimental findings, discusses effect sizes, and considers confounding variables (e.g., pre-existing mental health). |
| **Balanced Argument** | Only one side presented; no counter-argument. | Acknowledges an opposing view but does not engage with it substantively. | Presents a genuine counter-argument (e.g., social connection benefits, moderation effect) and weighs it against the main claim. | Balanced, well-integrated argument; student considers individual differences (age, gender, platform type) and concludes with a conditional judgement. |
| **Structure & Conclusion** | No clear structure; ideas are fragmented. | Some structure but conclusion merely restates the introduction. | Clear essay structure; conclusion reaches a justified verdict on the claim based on the evidence weighed. | Elegant structure; conclusion is proportionate, acknowledges uncertainty, and suggests what further evidence would be needed to strengthen the case. |

---

### Scoring Guide
* **13–16 Points:** High Distinction — Sophisticated evaluation of theory and evidence with methodological awareness.
* **9–12 Points:** Pass/Credit — Competent coverage of mechanisms and evidence with some critical evaluation.
* **5–8 Points:** Developing — Describes rather than evaluates; limited engagement with research quality.
* **0–4 Points:** Needs Improvement — Anecdotal reasoning; no psychological framework applied.
