## Essay Question

**Topic:** Climate Policy and Economic Trade-offs

**Question:** "Carbon taxes are the most efficient and equitable policy instrument for reducing greenhouse gas emissions." To what extent do you agree with this statement? In your answer, compare carbon taxes to at least one alternative policy mechanism and consider the distributional effects on different socioeconomic groups."

---

## Scoring Rubric

| Criteria | **1 - Beginning** | **2 - Developing** | **3 - Proficient** | **4 - Exemplary** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Understanding of Policy Instruments** | Carbon tax described incorrectly or conflated with other policies. | Carbon tax defined correctly but no meaningful comparison to alternatives. | Accurate explanation of carbon taxes and at least one alternative (e.g., cap-and-trade, regulatory standards, subsidies for renewables); key trade-offs identified. | Nuanced comparison of multiple instruments; student understands the theoretical basis for pricing externalities and can explain why different contexts may favour different tools. |
| **Efficiency vs. Equity Analysis** | Only efficiency or equity addressed, not both. | Both dimensions mentioned but treated separately without synthesis. | Analyses efficiency (incentive to reduce at lowest cost) and equity (regressive impact on low-income households) with concrete examples or evidence. | Sophisticated integration of efficiency and equity arguments; discusses revenue recycling options (e.g., dividend schemes, tax cuts) and their distributional consequences. |
| **Use of Evidence** | Purely theoretical or purely anecdotal with no real-world reference. | References a country or scheme (e.g., British Columbia, EU ETS) but without analytical use. | Draws on real-world evidence to support or challenge claims about effectiveness or equity. | Multiple well-chosen examples used analytically; student distinguishes between schemes that have succeeded and failed and explains why. |
| **Evaluative Judgement** | No conclusion or a conclusion that merely restates the question. | Reaches a verdict but it is not supported by the argument that precedes it. | Clear, supported judgement on the "to what extent" framing that engages with both sides. | Conditional, sophisticated verdict that acknowledges context-dependency (e.g., political feasibility, complementary policies needed) and the limits of any single instrument. |

---

### Scoring Guide
* **13–16 Points:** High Distinction — Rigorous economic and political analysis with balanced, well-evidenced judgement.
* **9–12 Points:** Pass/Credit — Competent coverage of instruments and trade-offs with some evaluation.
* **5–8 Points:** Developing — Descriptive; instruments explained but not critically compared.
* **0–4 Points:** Needs Improvement — Fundamental confusion between policy types or the concepts of efficiency and equity.
