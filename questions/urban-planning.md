## Essay Question

**Topic:** Urban Planning and the Remote Work Revolution

**Question:** "Analyze the long-term implications of the shift toward remote and hybrid work models on the infrastructure of major metropolitan areas. To what extent must city planners rethink the 'Central Business District' model to ensure urban sustainability and economic viability in the coming decade? Discuss with reference to transportation, housing, and public spaces."

---

## Scoring Rubric

This rubric evaluates the essay on a scale of **1 to 4** across four key dimensions.

| Criteria | **1 - Beginning** | **2 - Developing** | **3 - Proficient** | **4 - Exemplary** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Argument & Thesis** | Thesis is missing or unclear; does not directly address the prompt. | Thesis is present but broad; provides a basic response to the prompt. | Clear, focused thesis that addresses all parts of the prompt with a logical stance. | Sophisticated, nuanced thesis that demonstrates a deep understanding of urban complexity. |
| **Evidence & Analysis** | Claims are unsupported or rely solely on personal opinion. | Uses some general evidence, but analysis is superficial or repetitive. | Provides specific examples (e.g., transit data, zoning laws) with logical connections. | Integrates high-quality evidence with insightful analysis of cause and effect. |
| **Structure & Flow** | Lacks a logical progression; ideas are disjointed or confusing. | Basic introduction, body, and conclusion, though transitions may be clunky. | Well-organized with clear paragraph transitions and a logical flow of arguments. | Seamless organization; each point builds masterfully toward a compelling conclusion. |
| **Style & Mechanics** | Frequent errors in grammar, spelling, or tone that obscure meaning. | Occasional errors; tone is inconsistent or overly informal. | Proper academic tone; minor errors do not distract the reader. | Precise, engaging vocabulary; flawless mechanics and a professional, persuasive tone. |

---

### Scoring Guide
* **13–16 Points:** High Distinction – Demonstrates mastery of the subject and excellent rhetorical skill.
* **9–12 Points:** Pass/Credit – Meets the requirements with clear, logical communication.
* **5–8 Points:** Developing – Requires revision in evidence-gathering or structural clarity.
* **0–4 Points:** Needs Improvement – Fundamental misunderstanding of the prompt or significant writing gaps.
